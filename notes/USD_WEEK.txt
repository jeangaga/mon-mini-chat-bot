A. US WEEK — MACRO SYNTHESIS

The week resolved into a clearer inflation-constrained, stagflation-risk configuration rather than a growth-break regime. Growth data stayed mixed: private construction spending and mortgage-finance transmission deteriorated, national services momentum softened, and consumer psychology worsened, but regional manufacturing surveys stabilized or improved and the external balance improved. Labor did not crack in hard data — claims stayed low and continuing claims fell — but softer employment signals in CFNAI and the flash PMI showed some cooling at the margin.

The decisive weekly shift came from the inflation and expectations side. Q4 unit labor costs revised higher, import and export prices broadened sharply even before the full March war shock, PMI price gauges accelerated, and Michigan showed a worsening near-term inflation scare with only conditionally anchored long-run expectations. The resulting regime is not hard-data recession confirmation; it is restrictive-hold reinforcement under a worse growth/inflation mix.
