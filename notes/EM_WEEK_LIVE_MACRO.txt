The NBS highlighted a robust start to the year; industrial production accelerated to 6.3%, retail sales improved to 2.8%, and fixed-asset investment swung to +1.8% after a 2025 decline. Comment — Economist Layer

Infrastructure rose 11.4% and manufacturing 3.1%, while property investment still fell 11.1%. HF Take — Strategist Layer

This means India’s inflation constraint is becoming more pipeline-driven, not yet consumer-endogenous. State transition: deterioration

Context from prior regional live file:

For rates and FX, the message is conditional: India remains one of the stronger EM growth stories, but the inflation side is becoming less forgiving. State transition: deterioration

Context from prior regional live file:
The earlier Brazil sequence had moved from pipeline disinflation and weak Q4 growth toward mixed-but-resilient domestic activity, leaving Copom room to start easing. This should be read as a hawkish cut for BRL and front-end rates. The next question is no longer whether easing starts, but whether April can deliver 50bp or whether the cycle effectively stalls at 25bp increments.
