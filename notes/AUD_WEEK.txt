It removed the growth-weakness argument for inaction at the February RBA meeting and shifted the burden of proof onto the Q4 CPI quarterly aggregate.
Australia CPI (Nov, released 7 Jan 2026)
Why it matters: The grey-zone trimmed mean character of this print is analytically central. Headline disinflation was real, but the monthly trimmed mean momentum was not collapsing. Understanding why the print did not resolve the February decision — and what would have been needed to resolve it — is essential for reading the Q4 aggregate that follows in Block 2.
Australia PMI Flash (Jan, released 23 Jan 2026)
Why it matters: A composite of 55.5 at the start of 2026 is not consistent with an economy that needs rate relief. This print, combined with the labour report, established the activity backdrop that made the February hike a near-certainty by the close of this window.
