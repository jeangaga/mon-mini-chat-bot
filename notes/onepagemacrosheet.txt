AUSTRALIA — ONE-PAGE MACRO SHEET

Rolling dashboard window: 6 Mar 2026 to 20 Mar 2026
YTD arc window: 5 Jan 2026 to 20 Mar 2026
Last updated: 20 Mar 2026
Current regime label: hawkish continuation with rising internal resistance

ROLLING MACRO DASHBOARD
Growth: Leading momentum softened further; the Westpac-Melbourne Institute index fell for a second straight month in February and the six-month annualised pace was near flat.
Labor: Headline hiring held up, but quality deteriorated: February employment +48.9k, full-time -30k, part-time +79k, hours worked -0.2%, unemployment up to 4.3%.
Inflation: No new CPI print in-window; inflation remains proxied by the RBA’s view that domestic demand is still outstripping supply and inflation was already too high before the fuel shock.
Financial Conditions: Restriction increased again with a second consecutive 25bp RBA hike to 4.10%, leaving tighter settings hitting the economy as forward growth signals soften.
Policy Constraint: May remains live, but not locked; the 5–4 split, weaker leading pulse, and softer labour composition are the first visible constraints on a further hiking sequence.

SIGNAL TENSION CHECK
Main contradiction: The RBA is still tightening on domestic-capacity inflation logic while the forward-growth pulse and labour quality are softening at the same time.
Why it matters: The policy case is still hawkish in level terms, but the data are starting to validate the minority concern that further tightening may run into weakening transmission.
Implication: AUD keeps near-term relative-rate support, but over-tightening risk is now the primary monitor into May.

3 KEY RELEASES TO DIG INTO
RBA Cash Rate (17 Mar 2026): Second consecutive hike confirmed Australia as the only clear DM hiker, but the 5–4 split showed the cycle is closer to its limit than the headline move suggests.
Employment / Participation / Unemployment (19 Mar 2026): The report challenged the clean hawkish read because payroll growth came with weaker quality, lower hours, and unemployment above consensus.
Composite Leading Index (18 Mar 2026): A second consecutive negative print says late-2025 above-trend momentum has faded materially, sharpening the over-tightening question.

NEXT GATING CONDITION

The key test is whether Q1 inflation remains sticky enough to justify May tightening if labour quality and the leading index keep weakening.
A third consecutive soft leading signal alongside another full-time/hours deterioration would make the May case materially harder to hold.

YTD FROZEN ARC

Data window: 5 Jan 2026 to 23 Jan 2026
Pre-hike evidence accumulated but did not fully resolve the regime. Headline disinflation improved, yet underlying inflation stayed in a grey zone while labour re-tightened and PMIs accelerated. The macro mix shifted away from “possible pause” toward “hike increasingly hard to avoid,” with activity and labour doing most of the work.

Data window: 26 Jan 2026 to 6 Feb 2026
Inflation persistence resolved the grey zone upward and the RBA opened the hiking cycle. Q4 trimmed mean beat, the February hike to 3.85% was unanimous, and Bullock’s language framed the economy as above potential with financial conditions still somewhat accommodative. Australia became the clear DM policy outlier: not pausing, not cutting, but tightening.

Data window: 15 Feb 2026 to 6 Mar 2026
The post-hike assessment validated the RBA’s logic but also revealed early transmission. Q4 GDP beat and core inflation stayed sticky enough to keep May as the base case, while labour remained stable rather than breaking. At the same time, construction, approvals, credit, and the external sector softened, establishing the first real “stabilization rebuilt, but fast-cut hopes capped / transmission starting” phase.

Data window: 6 Mar 2026 to 20 Mar 2026
Hawkish continuation persisted, but with visibly rising internal resistance. The RBA hiked again to 4.10% on domestic demand-versus-supply grounds, yet the vote narrowed to 5–4, the leading index weakened for a second month, and labour quality deteriorated despite a strong employment headline. The regime is still “policy pivot confirmed” in the tightening direction, but it has transitioned into “better optics, same transmission failure risk” as over-tightening becomes the dominant question.

ENTERING NEXT MONITORING PERIOD

Key verification test: does sticky Q1 inflation still dominate, or do weaker labour quality and a softer leading pulse force the RBA from hawkish continuation into pause logic?

EUR — ONE-PAGE MACRO SHEET

Rolling dashboard window: 3 Mar 2026 to 20 Mar 2026
YTD arc window: 5 Jan 2026 to 20 Mar 2026
Last updated: 20 Mar 2026
Current regime label: growth shock + sticky services inflation + tighter ECB constraint

ROLLING MACRO DASHBOARD
Growth: clearly weaker; euro-area industrial data, German orders/ZEW, and Italian output all broke against the earlier stabilization story.
Labor: still not rolling over decisively at aggregate level, but the recent window gave little fresh labor support against the growth deterioration.
Inflation: not explosively higher, but sticky in the wrong places; euro-area core held 2.4%, services stayed firm, and Italy moved more clearly into services-led persistence.
Financial Conditions: effectively tighter; ECB rates were unchanged, but communication shifted toward higher uncertainty, upside inflation risk, and shock-management optionality.
Policy Constraint: materially worse; weaker growth no longer opens an easy dovish path because the inflation composition and energy shock keep the ECB cautious.
SIGNAL TENSION CHECK
Main contradiction: forward growth expectations broke sharply lower just as domestic inflation persistence and energy-shock upside risks kept policy from turning cleanly dovish.
Why it matters: the euro area is moving into a stagflationary mix, not a simple slowdown-with-disinflation regime.
Implication: weaker data alone are no longer enough to imply faster cuts; March-April inflation and activity must show whether the shock stays first-round or starts creating second-round persistence.
3 KEY RELEASES TO DIG INTO
Germany ZEW (17 Mar): the clearest forward-looking growth break; expectations collapsed and the survey itself pointed to rising inflation pressure.
Euro Area HICP Final (18 Mar): confirmed the ECB entered the shock with services still sticky and core still too firm for a clean dovish response.
ECB rates / communication (19 Mar): no rate move, but a real regime change in language toward uncertainty, upside inflation risk, and downside growth risk.
NEXT GATING CONDITION

March-April data must answer whether this is still a first-round energy shock layered onto soft growth, or the start of a more durable stagflationary regime via services, wage, and pricing spillovers.
The key checks are euro-area services/core inflation, German hard activity after the ZEW break, and whether trade/industry weakness spreads beyond the core.

YTD FROZEN ARC

Data window: 5 Jan–16 Jan
Disinflation confidence improved while growth stayed low-trend but stabilizing. Germany and euro-area inflation surprised softer, goods-side pressures eased, and hard activity stopped deteriorating outright, giving the ECB more comfort on inflation without creating a strong growth rebound.

Data window: 19 Jan–6 Feb
Inflation victory met growth fragility. Headline inflation moved below target and goods disinflation deepened, but retail, services momentum, and German production softened enough to open a dovish fork rather than confirm a clean soft landing.

Data window: 9 Feb–6 Mar
Stabilization rebuilt, but fast-cut hopes were capped. GDP, employment, and PMIs stabilized, yet February flash HICP/core/services re-firmed and upstream disinflation looked less clean, keeping the ECB in patience mode rather than reopening an easy easing path.

Data window: 9 Mar–13 Mar
Industrial hard-data reversed lower and the macro mix turned more stagflationary. Germany orders/production/trade, Italy output, and euro-area industrial production all disappointed, but inflation dispersion and energy-risk sensitivity prevented a straightforward dovish interpretation.

Data window: 17 Mar–20 Mar
The growth shock intensified and the ECB shifted into shock-management optionality. German ZEW collapsed, euro-area inflation stayed sticky in composition, and ECB communication moved out of benign-hold language toward a more constrained response function.

ENTERING NEXT MONITORING PERIOD

The verification test is whether the euro area remains in “weaker growth, sticky domestic inflation, constrained ECB” or whether incoming spring data soften enough to reopen a genuine easing window.
