Japan’s second week remained bifurcated but resilient enough to keep BOJ optionality alive. Japan's macro sequence across this window resolved into a hawkish hold with escalating normalization pressure — the most important single policy signal in the DM file for medium-term rate direction. No language was introduced that would signal a strategic retreat from normalization. The operative framing for the full window is therefore: services stable, capex durable, trade mixed, and the BoJ waiting through an oil-shock overlay without abandoning the direction of travel.
