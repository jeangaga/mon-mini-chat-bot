A. JPY WEEK — MACRO SYNTHESIS
Japan’s three-week tape resolved into a split-economy / BoJ-normalization-with-stagflation-risk regime. The household side stayed weak: February household spending missed badly, tertiary activity softened, services PMI slowed, chain-store sales contracted, and confidence fell sharply. But the corporate and external/capex side did not collapse: machinery orders rebounded strongly, Reuters Tankan non-manufacturing improved, exports stayed resilient, and flash manufacturing surged. The quality of that strength deteriorated, however, because manufacturing strength increasingly looked like supply-disruption front-loading rather than demand-led acceleration, while imports and terms-of-trade pressure worsened.
The inflation side became more important than the growth side for the BoJ. CGPI reaccelerated, nationwide CPI stopped getting cleaner, core-core stayed above 2%, and service PPI rose to 3.1%, the clearest domestic pricing signal in the file. That means Japan is not a simple weak-demand / dovish-BoJ story. It is a more uncomfortable mix: household demand softens, external balance quality worsens, but pipeline and services inflation strengthen enough to keep normalization alive. The resulting JPY regime is April hold / June-live: near-term BoJ patience, but a firmer medium-term hike path if services inflation and wage-price pass-through remain intact.
