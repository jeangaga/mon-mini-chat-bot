5) FX & Rates – Macro-Relative Construction (USD)

Rates (macro lens)

Base case: disinflation progress + sequential cooling in activity supports “pause with optionality,” not a mechanical easing glidepath.

Asymmetry: convexity remains to lower yields if labour softens cleanly, but expectations/credibility caps how fast the Fed can move.

Tail channel: term premium / external financing can tighten endogenously, so the rates story is reaction-function + risk premium, not just “data surprise.”

USD (macro-relative, conditional)
Sources of macro support: demand resilience keeps US growth premium from evaporating; Fed can justify holding if labour stabilises.
Sources of macro drag: narrowing rate differentials if the Fed eases more than peers; medium-horizon risk premia debates.
Conditions for narrative change:

USD-positive: sticky expectations + labour stabilisation ? longer hold, differentials persist.

USD-negative: claims trend higher + payroll weakness persists cleanly ? faster cuts, differentials compress.

Wall Street Consensus Lens (public) — structured & stress-tested

Macro consensus
The prevailing baseline remains late-cycle cooling / soft-landing rather than recession. Growth is expected to slow but stay positive; demand to decelerate but hold; inflation to improve but remain a “last mile” issue that keeps policy cautious.

Fed path consensus
Consensus converges on gradual easing, not a fast cutting cycle. The debate is “how many cuts” rather than “whether cuts happen,” with expectations/credibility as the binding constraint unless labour deterioration becomes persistent and clean.

Equities consensus
Equity strategists are broadly constructive but selective: earnings growth moderates but remains positive; valuation support is rate-path dependent; equities are implicitly long the soft-landing narrative and therefore most vulnerable to labour-driven downside surprises.

USD consensus
The dominant view is a gradually weaker USD over a 6–12 month horizon as differentials narrow and US exceptionalism fades, with important conditionality: tactical USD support can persist via risk premia/flows, and risk-off regimes can override the medium-horizon drift.
