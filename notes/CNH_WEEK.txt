A. CNH WEEK — MACRO SYNTHESIS

China’s concatenated window resolved into a supply-strong / demand-weak regime, now reinforced by a PBoC tape that confirms accommodative hold rather than imminent broad easing. The external and production side remained resilient: exports, imports and the trade surplus beat sharply, IP outperformed modestly, and AI / high-tech / equipment channels kept the supply side alive. But each subsequent domestic signal weakened the quality of that read: CPI stayed soft while PPI accelerated, TSF beat only through policy / bond support while private bank-credit internals disappointed, and the later activity batch showed retail contraction, sharp FAI deterioration and persistent property drag.

The policy implication is not overheating but targeted-support pressure under a narrower policy box. The PBoC / Beijing diagnosis is increasingly that the constraint is weak credit demand rather than scarce liquidity, pushing the easing baton toward fiscal and reducing the expected efficacy of near-term LPR cuts. CNH has an external-surplus cushion and structural support from firm fixings / yuan-internationalization measures, but the macro beta is capped by weak household demand, property stress, margin-squeeze risk and poor private-credit transmission. The week is therefore not broad China reflation; it is external / tech resilience alongside domestic demand deficiency, with policy support still needed but increasingly fiscal / targeted rather than broad monetary.
