A. CNH WEEK — MACRO SYNTHESIS

Over these two EM weeks, the China/CNH story resolved into a front-loaded headline-growth but internally weak-demand regime, not a clean reflation or clean CNH-positive turn. The first week showed the price mix worsening: PPI turned positive for the first time in more than three years while CPI undershot and fell m/m, confirming imported-cost pressure without household-demand absorption. The second week then completed the split: March credit missed across the board, exports slowed sharply while imports surged and compressed the surplus, and Q1 GDP beat at 5.0% only because industry, exports, infrastructure and fiscal support held up better than consumption. Retail slowed to 1.7%, investment missed, and unemployment edged up.

For CNH, that leaves the macro message cautious rather than outright crisis-like. Beijing can point to 5.0% growth and avoid immediate broad easing pressure, but the internal composition is too soft to support a clean currency-positive read. The regime is supply-side resilience, weak private demand, worse trade mix and targeted-support bias. That argues against a hard-landing CNH view, but it also caps enthusiasm for a sustained CNH strengthening story unless domestic demand, credit transmission and export breadth improve together.
