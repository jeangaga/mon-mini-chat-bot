A. SEK WEEK — MACRO SYNTHESIS

SEK over the supplied window was a weak-demand / Riksbank-reprieve file with a thin central-bank tape. The late-May data confirmed Sweden’s soft domestic-growth base: Q1 GDP contracted q/q, retail sales flattened after a downward revision, and consumer confidence remained below normal despite a marginal NIER bounce. The early-June activity tape improved the optics through April GDP indicator stabilization and a strong industrial / production-value rebound, but the quality was not clean because the industrial signal was value-based, partly price-flattered and externally supported rather than domestic-demand-led.

The key regime anchor is inflation. May CPI / CPIF confirmed the low-underlying-inflation profile: CPIF at 1.5% and CPIF ex energy at 0.5% remained well below the 2% target. That gives the Riksbank room to wait. The central-bank chatterbox does not create an in-window policy transition: no clean in-window monetary-policy speech was verified, the quiet period clipped the tail of the window, and the 17 Jun path-defining decision sits just outside the data window. The operative state is therefore a continuation of the 7 May hawkish-leaning hold: energy / Hormuz persistence and SEK weakness are the upside-inflation risks, while weak activity and low core inflation justify patience. SEK is a conditional stabilization currency, not a hawkish-policy currency.
