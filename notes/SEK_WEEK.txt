A. SEK WEEK — MACRO SYNTHESIS

SEK stayed in the cleanest conditional-stabilization regime inside the DM cohort. Activity was mixed rather than weak enough to force easing: Q1 GDP contracted and confidence softened, but March GDP and retail rebounded, housing expectations improved, manufacturing PMI looked strong, private production rose, and unemployment remained elevated but non-deteriorating. The quality of the activity signal remained uneven because PMI strength was partly delivery-time / supply-chain distorted and industrial production fell despite stronger broader private production.

The Riksbank tape reinforces that Sweden is not a simple dovish story; it is a “scope to wait” story. Thedéen’s pre-meeting framework explicitly legitimised wait-and-see because Sweden entered the shock with a low inflation starting point. The 7 May decision confirmed a fifth consecutive hold at 1.75%, with the Monetary Policy Update acknowledging both weaker growth / lower inflation outcomes and a higher tail risk from the Middle East supply shock. April CPIF was extremely soft at 0.8% y/y and CPIF ex-energy at 0.0%, but the downside was heavily food / VAT mechanical while fuel inflation remained strong. The decisive anchor was Prospera: 5-year CPIF expectations at 2.0% confirmed credibility and validated Riksbank patience. Final SEK regime: stabilization confirmed, with conditional flags preserved around Hormuz duration, ECB reaction function, SEK pass-through and the June MPR.
