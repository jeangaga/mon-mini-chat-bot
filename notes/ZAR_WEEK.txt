A. ZAR WEEK — MACRO SYNTHESIS

South Africa’s multi-week signal moved from conditional growth improvement into a clearer stagflation / SARB-constraint regime, with the central-bank tape confirming a genuine reaction-function inflection. Early data looked constructive: March retail sales were still resilient and the March leading indicator delivered the strongest forward-growth signal in the supplied South Africa tape, helped by real M1 and the interest-rate spread. But that improvement was progressively overtaken by the inflation-policy sequence: April CPI jumped from 3.1% to 4.0%, April PPI surprised sharply higher, and SARB converted the inflation shock into an actual 25bp hike, with higher inflation forecasts and lower growth forecasts.

The final June data resolved the tension negatively. The Standard Bank whole-economy PMI fell back below 50, output and new orders contracted, and input / output price inflation accelerated sharply, confirming that the fuel / Middle East shock was damaging real activity while raising price pressure. The reserve print was benign and prevents a Turkey-style balance-of-payments stress read, but it does not repair the domestic regime. The SARB chatterbox hardens the policy read: Kganyago framed the hike as pre-emptive target defense under the new 3% regime, argued the post-hike stance was still less restrictive than in March on a real-rate basis, and ruled out reverting to the old 3–6% band. The final ZAR macro state is therefore stagflation-lite with credible reserves and an active hike tail: pressure is located in activity / inflation / SARB trade-off, not external-buffer depletion.
