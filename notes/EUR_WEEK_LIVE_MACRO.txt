A. EUR WEEK — MACRO SYNTHESIS

The EUR week ended with the regional regime shifting further into a stagflationary warning zone. Growth signals deteriorated across the forward-looking and household side: euro-area consumer confidence shocked lower, France flash activity contracted, Germany Ifo expectations fell sharply, Germany GfK and Italy confidence weakened, and the bloc PMI mix showed near-stalling activity with weaker services and softer new orders. Spain remained the main resilience pocket through strong pre-shock Q4 GDP and tourism continuity, but that strength was backward-looking and insufficient to offset the broader regional slowdown signal.

The inflation side did not relieve the growth weakness; it complicated it. PMIs, France household inflation expectations and Spain’s March CPI/HICP all pointed to renewed price pressure, even if Spain printed softer than feared. Financial conditions did not show fracture in the February M3 data, but the effective policy mix tightened because weaker growth arrived alongside renewed inflation pressure rather than disinflation. The resulting weekly macro regime is weaker forward growth, broader confidence deterioration and a more binding ECB constraint rather than a clean easing setup.

B. EUR MACRO SIGNAL SCOREBOARD

Growth: -
Supporting evidence:
The week’s decisive releases were growth-negative. Euro-area flash consumer confidence fell sharply to -16.3, euro-area composite PMI dropped to 50.5 with services at 50.1, France composite fell to 48.3, Germany Ifo expectations dropped from 90.5 to 86.0, and France manufacturing climate fell below average. Spain Q4 GDP at 0.8% q/q and 2.7% y/y was the main offset, but it was explicitly pre-shock baseline strength rather than fresh forward momentum.

Labor: ~
Supporting evidence:
Labor softened only incrementally in the supplied file rather than through a full labor-market break. France Unemp Class-A SA rose to 3,108.2k from 3,090.5k, euro-area and national survey blocks referenced softer employment or weaker demand conditions, and household/business confidence deterioration raises labor downside risk. But there was no decisive regional labor-market collapse release in the week, so the labor signal moved softer without being fully resolved.

Inflation: -
Supporting evidence:
The inflation constraint re-intensified. The euro-area PMI block explicitly described “stagflation alarm bells,” with strong input-cost pressure and delivery disruption; Germany manufacturing input prices were highlighted as rising at the fastest pace in roughly three years; France consumer confidence showed a sharp jump in future price expectations; and Spain March HICP/CPI accelerated to 3.3% y/y. Spain undershot consensus and therefore softened the immediate inflation scare, but the weekly inflation message was still re-acceleration rather than relief.

Financial Conditions: ~
Supporting evidence:
The week did not show outright financial fracture. Euro-area M3 growth slowed only modestly to 3.0%, household loans were stable at 3.0%, and NFC loan growth edged up to 2.9%, which the live file treated as a functional pre-shock monetary baseline rather than a stress event. At the same time, multiple survey/commentary blocks referenced worse financial conditions, higher borrowing costs and rising uncertainty, so the signal is tighter in felt conditions but not yet visibly broken in hard monetary data.

Policy Constraint: -
Supporting evidence:
The ECB constraint hardened materially. The week combined collapsing confidence, weaker France activity, worsening Germany forward expectations and renewed inflation pressure in surveys and Spain prices. Several blocks explicitly stated that this was not a clean dovish growth slowdown because weaker activity was arriving alongside renewed inflation pressure, leaving the ECB more trapped in a stagflationary bind rather than reopening an easy easing path.

More structurally, Germany enters this shock with a weaker supply side than in 2022 because part of the least productive energy-intensive base has already been lost. That means a fresh energy shock hits a less resilient production structure. State transition: deterioration.

Context from prior regional live file

The prior EUR live file had already flagged Germany as the epicentre of the forward-looking deterioration after ZEW expectations collapsed. Ifo now validates that earlier warning with Germany’s premier business survey: the recovery narrative is weakening further, not stabilizing.

For the reaction function, this is a more durable growth-negative signal than the PMI manufacturing beat. It tells you that even if present activity has not fully rolled over, firms are already discounting a weaker Q2 path.
