Italy — Consumer Prices Final / HICP Final (Feb)

Reuters Data

Consumer Prices Final YY: 1.5% | Prior: 1.6% | Poll: 1.6% | Surprise: -0.10%
CPI (EU Norm) Final MM: 0.5% | Prior: 0.6% | Poll: 0.6% | Surprise: -0.10%
CPI (EU Norm) Final YY: 1.5% | Prior: 1.6% | Poll: 1.6% | Surprise: -0.10%

Professional Market Commentary

Official ISTAT final data revised both NIC and HICP inflation slightly below the flash, with annual inflation at 1.5% and the main acceleration still coming from transport services, recreation/personal care services, and unprocessed food, while regulated and non-regulated energy remained deflationary. The scout’s construction layer is directionally consistent with the official release: the headline revision is modest, but the mix is less benign than the 1.5% headline alone suggests.

Comment — Economist Layer

The policy-relevant signal is not the 0.1pp downward revision to the final headline. It is the internal re-acceleration of domestic inflation: core inflation excluding energy and unprocessed food jumped to 2.4% from 1.7%, inflation excluding energy rose to 2.5% from 1.9%, services accelerated to 3.6% from 2.5%, while goods stayed at -0.2%. That widens the services-goods gap to 3.8pp and materially weakens the “Italy as clean periphery disinflation” story. Headline HICP is still below the euro area aggregate, but the composition has clearly firmed.

State transition: reversal

Context from prior regional live file:

The prior EUR live file still framed Italy’s inflation backdrop as low and broadly non-threatening for the ECB, with the earlier Italy CPI/HICP final block sitting at 1.2% y/y and described as only marginally dovish in aggregate. February’s 1.5% final, combined with the jump in core and services, marks a meaningful move away from that earlier benign profile.

HF Take — Strategist Layer

For the ECB, Italy is no longer reinforcing the easy “periphery disinflation keeps aggregate inflation tame” narrative. This is still not a standalone hawkish trigger, but it does raise the floor under regional core/services persistence. In cross-release terms, it fits better with euro area services stickiness than with a renewed disinflation break.

Germany — ZEW Economic Sentiment / Current Conditions (Mar)
Release Date: 17 Mar 2026 | Local Time: 11:00

Reuters Data

ZEW Economic Sentiment: -0.5 | Prior: 58.3 | Poll: 39.0 | Surprise: -39.50
ZEW Current Conditions: -62.9 | Prior: -65.9 | Poll: -67.3 | Surprise: 4.40

Professional Market Commentary

The official ZEW framing is explicit: expectations collapsed as the Middle East conflict pushed up energy prices and inflation pressure. ZEW President Achim Wambach said the shock raises the risk that Germany’s emerging recovery slows, and the official release also stressed that the panel does not expect a quick resolution. The scout tape’s geopolitical-shock interpretation is therefore strongly anchored by the official source rather than being an AI-added narrative.

Comment — Economist Layer

This is a severe forward-looking growth shock, not a broad confirmation that current activity has already rolled over. Expectations fell 58.8 points to -0.5, while current conditions improved 3.0 points to -62.9. The split matters: backward-looking conditions were still stabilising, but forward expectations broke sharply. The official release also identifies the vulnerable core: chemicals/pharma, autos, mechanical engineering, steel/metals, and construction. Most importantly for policy, about 80% of respondents now expect inflationary pressures to build in both Germany and the eurozone, while eurozone expectations themselves fell to -8.5 and the eurozone current-situation measure dropped to -29.9. That is a classic stagflationary survey mix: weaker growth expectations plus higher inflation expectations.

State transition: deterioration

Context from prior regional live file:

The prior EUR live file had February ZEW as fragile stabilisation: expectations were still positive at 58.3 and current conditions were improving from very weak levels. March effectively erases that interpretation. The earlier “cautious recovery” reading no longer survives contact with the new expectations collapse.

HF Take — Strategist Layer

This is the most important growth signal of the week so far. It does not force immediate ECB action by itself, but it sharply worsens the forward activity backdrop while simultaneously hardening the inflation-expectations problem. For rates and FX, the message is not “ECB cut soon”; it is “policy optionality narrows because the shock is stagflationary rather than purely growth-negative.”

Euro Zone — HICP Final / HICP-X F,E,A&T Final (Feb)
Release Date: 18 Mar 2026 | Local Time: 11:00

Reuters Data

HICP Final MM: 0.6% | Prior: -0.6% | Poll: 0.7% | Surprise: -0.10%
HICP Final YY: 1.9% | Prior: 1.9% | Poll: 1.9% | Surprise: 0.00%
HICP-X F,E,A&T Final MM: 0.8% | Prior: 0.8% | Poll: 0.8% | Surprise: 0.00%
HICP-X F,E,A&T Final YY: 2.4% | Prior: 2.4% | Poll: 2.4% | Surprise: 0.00%

Professional Market Commentary

Officially, the final release confirms the flash rather than changing it. Eurostat kept headline inflation at 1.9% and core at 2.4%. The official composition remains the same as in the flash narrative: services made the largest positive contribution to annual inflation, while energy stayed negative. The scout’s construction layer is therefore correctly centred on services persistence rather than on any final-release revision story.

Comment — Economist Layer

The incremental information content of the final is low, but the macro message remains important. January’s 1.7% headline trough is now followed by a confirmed February rebound to 1.9%, while core stays at 2.4%. Under the surface, services re-accelerated to 3.4% from 3.2%, non-energy industrial goods rose to 0.7% from 0.4%, and energy remained negative at -3.1%, contributing -0.30pp to the annual rate. So the euro area is still benefiting from energy disinflation, but the non-energy core mix is not easing cleanly. This is why the final is not “dovish confirmation” even though the headline is still below 2%.

State transition: no material change

Context from prior regional live file:

The prior EUR live file had already treated the February HICP flash as the strongest anti-fast-cut signal in the EUR block, with 1.9% headline and 2.4% core pushing against aggressive easing assumptions. The final does not alter that interpretation; it hard-confirms it.

HF Take — Strategist Layer

This keeps the ECB trapped in patience rather than reopening a dovish window. The important point is not that the final surprised; it did not. The important point is that the euro area entered the current shock with services still sticky and with goods disinflation already fading at the margin. That makes any future energy-led inflation re-acceleration harder to ignore.

ECB Refinancing Rate: 2.15% | Prior: 2.15% | Poll: 2.15%

Professional Market Commentary

The official ECB schedule confirms 19 March as a projection meeting, with the rate decision at 14:15 CET and staff projections at 15:45 CET. The ECB’s key-rate pages continue to show the unchanged structure of 2.00% on the deposit facility, 2.15% on main refinancing operations, and 2.40% on the marginal lending facility. The last fully anchored official policy baseline, from the February meeting accounts, still described inflation as stabilising around target in the medium term, with January headline inflation pushed below target by negative energy inflation.

Comment — Economist Layer

At the instrument level, this is continuity. The analytical significance comes from timing, not from the rate setting itself: the meeting arrives after the German ZEW expectations collapse and after euro area February inflation confirmed that services remain sticky. Without the full official March statement/projection text in hand, the only hard conclusion that can be locked is that the ECB did not validate any immediate easing response to the shock. That preserves the interpretation that the Governing Council still sees the inflation constraint as materially binding.

State transition: continuation

Context from prior regional live file:

The prior EUR live file was already leaning against aggressive easing because February flash HICP surprised higher and services mattered more than the headline. Relative to that backdrop, an unchanged March decision preserves the instrument stance and does not reopen the earlier benign-disinflation reading.

HF Take — Strategist Layer

For now, the ECB is signalling restraint rather than action. That is consistent with a reaction function in which growth fears are rising but inflation credibility still dominates. The market takeaway is not hawkishness in the classic sense; it is that the bar for cuts remains higher than a pure growth scare would normally imply.
