A. TWD WEEK — MACRO SYNTHESIS

Taiwan was the clean positive outlier in the three-week EM tape: the macro signal was not just resilient, but externally explosive. CPI undershot materially and remained below 2%, while exports, imports, export orders and industrial production all confirmed that AI, semiconductors, servers and electronics are still overwhelming the negative external shock. The key distinction is that Taiwan’s strength is not a domestic overheating story: the jobless rate barely moved, labor-market signal content stayed low, and the real impulse came almost entirely from the external tech cycle.

The resulting TWD regime is therefore high-quality but asymmetric: very strong external-growth and balance-of-payments support, soft inflation, and limited near-term CBC tightening pressure. The risk has shifted from “is the AI cycle still alive?” to “how much of this is durable capex extension versus forward-pulled ordering, and when does sequential momentum fade?” For TWD, this is still a supportive macro mix, but increasingly dependent on the persistence and breadth of AI-tech demand rather than broad domestic acceleration.
