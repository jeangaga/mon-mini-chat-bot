Professional Market Commentary

The official StatCan release showed GDP up 0.1% in January after 0.2% in December, driven by goods-producing industries, while services were flat; Reuters highlighted the same point and added that the flash estimate points to another 0.2% gain in February. That means Canada currently looks more resilient than some DM peers, but not because domestic breadth is especially strong; it is because the energy/goods side is cushioning weakness elsewhere.
State transition: stabilisation.

The prior DM live file had Canada on a softer-inflation / BoC-hold trajectory, with housing still rate-sensitive and policy bias no longer hawkish. Constructive for the BoC in the sense that policymakers can focus a little more on inflation/oil risk and a little less on imminent growth breakage.
