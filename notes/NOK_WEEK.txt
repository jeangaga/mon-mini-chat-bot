A. NOK WEEK — MACRO SYNTHESIS

NOK closes the concatenated window as a restrictive-hold / policy-constrained file. The macro tape is not a simple “Norway strong” story: mainland GDP remains modest, Regional Network signals activity cooling, and capacity utilisation / recruitment difficulty are easing. But the domestic-slack argument is not strong enough to open a dovish Norges Bank path because labour tightened, retail stabilized, and core inflation re-accelerated.

The key in-window inflation release is May CPI. Headline CPI eased to 3.1% y/y from 3.4%, but the headline improvement is partly tax-flattered and is not the policy signal. CPI-ATE rose to 3.4% y/y versus a 3.2% poll, and monthly core beat expectations. That validates the restrictive stance and keeps higher-for-longer intact.

The central-bank tape makes the reaction function clearer. Norges Bank entered the window after an out-of-consensus 25bp hike to 4.25% on 6 May, with inflation described as too high and the March path still pointing to 4.25–4.50% by end-2026. Inside the window there were no individual speakers; Norges Bank’s one-voice regime and the approach of the 18 June MPR left the tape speechless. The actual in-window policy inputs were the same two-way data: core inflation stickiness keeps the additional-hike option alive, while Regional Network cooling removes the case for a consecutive hike.

For NOK, the implication is supportive but conditional. NOK is not just an oil-beta trade: the more durable support comes from domestic/core policy constraint. But the consecutive-hike tail is fading because activity is cooling. The correct state is restrictive hold into a path-defining 18 June MPR: higher-for-longer intact, hike tail alive, but no clean back-to-back tightening signal.
