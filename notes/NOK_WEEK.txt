A. NOK WEEK — MACRO SYNTHESIS

NOK moved into the clearest Nordic active-tightening regime, and the Norges Bank scout confirms that the May hike was the execution of a committed March policy path rather than a one-off surprise. Norway’s tape is much more Norges Bank-validating than Sweden’s: registered unemployment fell to 2.1%, housing prices accelerated to 3.8% y/y, manufacturing output rebounded 2.0% m/m, Norges Bank raised to 4.25%, and April CPI then validated the decision ex post through a higher core path even as headline CPI eased.

The key policy distinction is that Norges Bank’s concern is domestic/core inflation, wages and capacity, not only the imported energy shock. Wolden Bache’s message was that the rate rise was not only due to the war, while CPI-ATE re-accelerated to 3.2% y/y and 0.7% m/m. Growth is not booming, credit growth is moderating, and the hike is better read as bringing forward the March path rather than launching a new aggressive cycle. But the data mix leaves Norges Bank with less room to sound dovish than most DM peers. Final NOK regime: hike delivered, hike validated, second hike conditional. NOK support is valid versus SEK and wait-and-see DM currencies, but follow-through now depends on June MPR, May CPI, wage data, NOK pass-through and whether Hormuz / oil risk persists.

The release confirms that headline disinflation is not enough to validate underlying disinflation. Norway’s inflation issue is domestic/core stickiness, wages and broad pass-through, not just headline energy noise.

NOK strengthening after the hike helps via imported inflation, but can also reduce the need for aggressive follow-through if sustained.

Policy Constraint: +
