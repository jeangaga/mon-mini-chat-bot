1. Scope & Run
Tag: US_MACRO_DAILY

Language: English only (headlines + commentary).

Run time (Paris):

13:00 CET: pre-US open / data preview.

22:00 CET: post-close wrap (if you want two runs; otherwise keep 13:00).

Each run overwrites/updates the same JSON for the day + one text note.

2. Text Note Structure (human-readable feed)
(0) Header snapshot (5–7 bullets)
At the very top:

1–2 bullets: macro theme (growth, inflation, labor).

1 bullet: Fed pricing (next 2–3 meetings: x bp of cuts/hikes).

1 bullet: UST curve (2y / 10y, real 10y).

1 bullet: USD (DXY + EURUSD / USDJPY short mention).

Optional 1 bullet: risk tone (SPX/NDX, credit spreads).

(1) Today’s US releases – calendar style (hedge-fund format)
Block title:

Today’s US releases (Paris AM snapshot)
(Times ET; “—” = not out yet; “Paused” = gov. blackout)

For each item:

Line 1: Name (Period) – Time ET

Line 2: Actual: x | Cons.: y | Prev.: z

Line 3: 2 lines of why it matters / HF angle.

Core list (always monitored):

NFP, unemployment rate, participation, avg hourly earnings.

CPI (headline & core, supercore/services if available).

PCE / Core PCE (monthly and deflator).

Retail sales (+ core control group).

ISM Manufacturing + Services (esp. new orders, employment, prices).

GDP (advance/second/final) and underlying demand.

JOLTS job openings, quits.

Initial & continuing jobless claims (if out).

Housing: housing starts & permits, existing / new home sales, pending home sales.

(2) “Minor” but hedge-fund-watched data
Block title:

Second-tier / high-frequency data (still price-relevant)

Use short bullets grouped by theme:

Labor & wages

Challenger job cuts

ADP employment

NFIB small business (jobs, comp plans, credit).

Activity / demand

Regional Fed surveys (Empire, Philly, Richmond, Dallas, Kansas, Chicago PMI).

Durable goods orders (core capital goods ex-aircraft).

Factory orders.

Industrial production & capacity utilisation.

Housing & credit

MBA mortgage applications (levels, % change, refi vs purchase).

Construction spending.

Case-Shiller home prices.

Inflation / costs

PPI details (core goods vs services, pipeline pressures).

Import/export prices.

HF / “alt-data-style” mentions

You don’t invent numbers; you just pull what’s public:

Atlanta Fed GDPNow latest print.

Any widely quoted card-spending / bank trackers (JPM/BofA consumer spending if the press quotes them).

Gasoline price trends if in news.

Each bullet: 1 line of result + 1 line of so-what.

(3) Fed policy & speakers tracker
Block title:

Fed – speeches, minutes, and pricing

Sub-sections:

(3.1) Fed pricing (from futures / OIS via news)
1–2 bullets:

Implied probability for next meeting (cut/hold/hike).

Total bp priced over next 12 months.

(3.2) Fed speakers (HF angle)

For each speaker today:

HH:MM ET – Name (role, voter/non-voter) – venue

Topic: …. Tone: hawkish / neutral / dovish vs last dots.

1 line: market-relevant quote / emphasis (if any) – no invented quotes, only what you can find.

(3.3) Documents / events

FOMC minutes, SEP, Financial Stability Report, BTFP/discount window usage (if in news).

(4) Hedge-fund followed “risk/flow” signals
Block title:

Positioning & risk indicators (HF focus)

You can’t see actual HF books, but you can track what they watch and what’s public:

CFTC positioning (weekly): net specs in USD, UST futures, major FX, equity indices, crude.

Credit & vol:

CDX IG / HY spread moves (direction / magnitude).

MOVE index (UST vol).

Equity put-call ratio, skew mentions.

Liquidity / balance sheet (if in news):

Fed H.4.1 balance sheet direction.

ON RRP, reserves trends if highlighted.

Each as short bullets, explicitly sourced (“CFTC weekly,” “press citing prime brokerage data,” etc.) – and if nothing new, say: “No fresh CFTC/prime brokerage updates beyond last week’s positioning.”

(5) Market reaction – USD & rates
Block title:

Market reaction – USD & US rates

Focus on macro-relevant assets, not micro:

Rates:

UST 2y, 5y, 10y (change on day, intraday reaction vs key data).

5y5y breakeven / 10y breakeven if press mentions.

FX:

DXY.

EURUSD, USDJPY, USDCNH short comment.

Optional line: SPX / NDX and sector colour only if macro-driven move (e.g. “rates-led tech selloff”).

Format: 3–6 bullets max; each one ties price move back to the data or Fed.
