  ‚Ä¢ a new source of macro risk,

  ‚Ä¢ or no material change.

The section must then include a rolling assessment over the past 1 to 3 months of the political regime relevant to macro:

The section must end with a concise hedge-fund synthesis stating:

- Do not infer positioning from price action.

----------------------------------------------------

The FX & Rates ‚Äì Macro Attribution section must translate macro and policy signals
into market-relevant implications, while remaining strictly macro-driven

This section explains how the current macro regime and central-bank reaction function

‚ÄúGiven the macro and policy setup, what should matter for FX and rates?‚Äù

This section must not:

- infer conclusions from market performance,

2) Dual-Horizon Framework (Mandatory)

The FX & Rates attribution must be structured along two horizons:

a) Short-Term Attribution (Signal Layer)

- Link recent macro data releases and central-bank communications
  (from the current run window) to:
  ‚Ä¢ near-term rates expectations (front-end, curve shape),
  ‚Ä¢ FX directional or relative biases.

  ‚Ä¢ reinforce existing macro-driven expectations,
  ‚Ä¢ introduce short-term uncertainty,
  ‚Ä¢ or fail to change the macro-driven outlook.

b) Structural Attribution (Rolling 1‚Äì3 Month Perspective)

- Anchor FX and rates implications in the rolling macro regime and
  the central bank‚Äôs reaction function over the past 1‚Äì3 months.

- Focus on:
  ‚Ä¢ policy-rate floor or ceiling,
  ‚Ä¢ duration of the policy plateau,
  ‚Ä¢ asymmetry of risks (cuts vs hikes),
  ‚Ä¢ relative macro dynamics versus key peers (for FX).

3) Rates-Specific Rules

- Discuss rates primarily through:
  ‚Ä¢ front-end policy expectations,
  ‚Ä¢ curve dynamics (steepening / flattening),
  ‚Ä¢ and risk asymmetry.

- Do not anchor analysis to specific yield levels unless necessary

4) FX-Specific Rules

- FX analysis must be relative by construction
  (domestic macro vs foreign macro / policy).

- Explicitly state:
  ‚Ä¢ sources of macro support,
  ‚Ä¢ sources of macro drag,
  ‚Ä¢ and conditions under which the FX narrative would change.

- Avoid directional claims unless they are clearly justified

5) Consistency with Other Sections

- FX & Rates attribution must be consistent with:
  ‚Ä¢ the Executive Summary,
  ‚Ä¢ the Top-Down Macro assessment,
  ‚Ä¢ and the Central Bank Speaker Tape synthesis.

- If FX or rates implications appear muted or range-bound,

6) Political Consistency Rule (Mandatory)

When a ‚ÄúPolitical Event with market impact‚Äù section is present,

Rules:
- FX and rates implications must reflect the dominant political macro transmission channel

- If political developments materially alter macro risk perception,
  this must be acknowledged explicitly in FX and/or rates attribution.

Prohibited behaviour:
