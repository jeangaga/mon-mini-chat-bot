United States — Employment Trends Index (Mar)
Release Date: 6 Apr 2026 | Local Time: 16:00
Importance: **

Reuters Data

Employment Trends: 105.72 | Prior: 105.37 | Revised: 105.84

Professional Market Commentary

Your scout and the official Conference Board framing align: this was read as a modest deterioration in a still-healthy labor-leading index rather than a break, but one with explicit forward caution on employer hiring behavior. Mitchell Barnes’ emphasis was that job seekers still face a challenging market and that rising geopolitical uncertainty could reinforce employer hesitancy.

Comment — Economist Layer

The signal is not the absolute level but the directional turn. March slipped below the revised February level, and five of eight ETI components contributed negatively, including “jobs hard to get,” firms unable to fill positions, industrial production, and real manufacturing and trade sales. Against that, claims, openings, and temp-help hiring still contributed positively, so this is not a collapse signal. It is better read as incipient labor-demand hesitation that sits comfortably alongside the weak ISM services employment reading. State transition: deterioration.

ISM N-Mfg New Orders: 60.6 | Prior: 58.6
ISM N-Mfg Prices Paid: 70.7 | Prior: 63.0

Professional Market Commentary

This is the week’s anchor release and your scout captures the tape correctly. Official ISM and Reuters both frame the report as continued services expansion with materially worse composition: activity slowed, employment fell into contraction, new orders were very strong, and prices paid jumped to the highest since October 2022 amid higher oil, fuel, shipping, and conflict-related disruption. The market narrative is therefore not “services rolled over,” but “services stayed alive while the inflation constraint re-tightened.”

Comment — Economist Layer

The internal hierarchy matters more than the headline. PMI at 54.0 still implies expansion, and new orders at 60.6 argue against a clean demand break. But business activity fell 6.0 points, employment dropped to 45.2, supplier deliveries slowed further, and prices paid surged 7.7 points to 70.7. Inventories and inventory sentiment staying in expansion reinforce your scout’s precautionary stock-building read rather than outright retrenchment. That makes this a stagflation-adjacent services print: demand still firm enough to keep the sector expanding, but cost pressure and hiring hesitation have clearly worsened. State transition: deterioration.
