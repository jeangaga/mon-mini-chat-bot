Breadth: autos, furniture, electronics, building materials → broad-based discretionary participation

Underlying real momentum ~0.4–0.6% m/m equivalent

Interpretation:

Consumer is resilient, not overheating
Energy shock has not yet compressed spending
Tax refunds are temporarily smoothing the shock

Forward structure:

Q2–Q3 risk: compression once temporary buffers fade
Current strength is partly timing-supported, not income-driven

State transition: continuation — stronger resilience within a stagflationary structure

Context from prior regional live file:
The prior USD sequence had already established a “resilient but fragile” consumer framework, with February interpreted as a temporary improvement rather than a clean re-acceleration. March strengthens the resilience leg of that framework: spending is holding up more than expected, but remains supported by temporary factors and exposed to a deteriorating real-income backdrop.
