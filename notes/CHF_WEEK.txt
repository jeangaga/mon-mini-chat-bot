SIGNAL TENSION CHECK
Main contradiction: Manufacturing PMI surged 3.9pp above consensus to 49.7 — removing the most pessimistic growth read — while retail sales softened from 2.8% to -1.1% YY, pointing to a household demand picture that deteriorated sharply from the prior month despite the manufacturing stabilisation.
Why it matters: The divergence between manufacturing stabilisation and retail softening is the key interpretive tension for the SNB's growth assessment. If manufacturing leads retail (as the PMI improvement implies forward demand is recovering), the growth picture may stabilise without requiring additional SNB easing. If retail leads (as the demand softening implies the consumer channel is not validating the manufacturing survey), the growth picture remains fragile and SNB dovish bias deepens.
Implication: SNB holds with a dovish bias. The Feb 15 – Mar 6 data block will provide the first comprehensive post-January activity assessment with GDP, KOF, retail, and PMI all arriving together. That cluster is the next meaningful Switzerland data event in the file.
