A. SWITZERLAND WEEK — MACRO SYNTHESIS
The three weeks from 15 February to 6 March delivered the most comprehensive Switzerland data window in the full monitoring file, and its net message was consistent with the baseline established in Block 1: soft current activity with a modestly better forward signal, near-zero inflation preserved, and the SNB in a low-inflation environment with no new catalyst forcing a policy change in either direction. The Q4 GDP print was the centrepiece: 0.1% QQ versus 0.2% consensus — a small miss on an already-soft number — with YY at 0.7% above the 0.5% prior but below the broader recovery expectations. The KOF leading indicator improved to 104.2 from 103.3 and above the 103.0 consensus, providing the one genuinely positive forward signal of the block and preventing the GDP miss from creating a clean recession narrative.
The activity picture elsewhere in the block was weak. January retail sales collapsed to -1.1% YY from +2.8% prior — the sharpest single domestic demand deterioration in the monitoring window, extending the softening visible in the November data from Block 1. Manufacturing PMI deepened contraction to 47.4 from 48.8 prior, missing the 50.0 consensus badly and confirming that the December PMI rebound was a one-month bounce rather than a sustained recovery. Unemployment edged up to 3.0% from 2.9%, a small but directionally negative labour signal. CPI for February rebounded modestly on the monthly measure but annual inflation stayed at 0.1% YY — barely positive and consistent with the SNB's structural low-inflation regime. Forex reserves declined marginally, consistent with no active intervention signal. Official reserve assets were stable. The net regime is: growth soft and not recovering, forward signal modestly better, inflation near-zero and stable, SNB dovish bias preserved without new urgency in either direction.
