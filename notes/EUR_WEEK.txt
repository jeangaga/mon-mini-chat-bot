EUR moved from weak-growth / firmer-headline-risk into an active stagflation nowcast. The weekly sequence was unusually coherent: German PPI loaded the upstream energy-cost pipeline, ZEW and Ifo confirmed a broad German shock, euro-area and French confidence broke lower, and the flash PMIs converted the regime from vulnerability into contraction, with services carrying the demand damage while manufacturing was flattered by front-loading and safety-stock behavior. The ECB read-through is therefore not cleanly dovish: hawkish hold remains supported by the inflation pipeline, but the communication burden rose materially because the growth side is now broad enough to make the bloc’s April tape a synchronized stagflation signal.
