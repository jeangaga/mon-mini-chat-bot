A. MEXICO WEEK — MACRO SYNTHESIS
Mexico’s January window moved from cautious disinflation with soft industrial momentum into a much sharper stagflationary bind. The opening CPI cluster confirmed that headline disinflation was still broadly on track: December CPI y/y eased to 3.69%, below consensus, and headline m/m surprised lower. But the core detail immediately limited the dovish read. Core CPI m/m rose to 0.41% from 0.19%, showing that underlying inflation was not yet cooperating with the headline improvement and that services/core persistence remained the binding Banxico constraint.
The growth side then deteriorated in two steps. November industrial output remained negative on an annual basis at -0.8% y/y, even though the m/m rebound reduced the immediate hard-landing tail. That was already a soft-growth signal, but the decisive deterioration came with November IGAE on 23 January: -0.1% y/y versus +1.0% consensus and +1.7% prior. That shifted the Mexico read from “industrial softness with headline disinflation” to “genuine activity shock with sticky core inflation.”
The resulting regime as of 30 January was therefore not a clean easing setup. Growth was weak enough to strengthen the case for lower rates, but core inflation was too sticky to allow Banxico to respond aggressively. MXN carry remained supported by the still-high policy-rate differential, but the quality of that carry deteriorated: if growth weakness persisted while core inflation stayed above the comfort zone, Banxico would face a harder credibility-versus-activity trade-off.
