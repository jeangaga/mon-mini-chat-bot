A. GBP WEEK — MACRO SYNTHESIS

The three-week UK tape resolved into a classic stagflationary false-dawn sequence. February hard activity looked much stronger than feared, with GDP, services and industrial output beating materially, but that strength was explicitly pre-war and was progressively undermined by more current signals: final March PMIs moved to near-stall, construction and all-sector activity slipped into contraction, housing surveys deteriorated, April confidence collapsed, and March retail was flattered by fuel stockpiling rather than genuine real-consumption acceleration. The April flash PMI beat then looked like the sharpest composition trap: stronger headline activity, but driven by front-loading, delivery-time distortions and supply-scare purchasing rather than durable demand.

The binding policy signal was inflation and margin pressure, not clean growth strength. CPI was less alarming than the headline because core eased and services were distorted by airfares, but PPI inputs surged, PMI input costs hit record pressure, and construction/input-cost measures showed firms absorbing a cost shock before full consumer pass-through. Labor softened beneath a false-positive unemployment headline: payrolls were negative, vacancies fell, inactivity rose, and private-sector regular pay slowed. The resulting GBP regime is BoE trapped / cuts delayed: enough forward softness to challenge growth optimism, but too much pipeline inflation and import-cost pressure to validate an easy easing narrative.

B. GBP MACRO SIGNAL SCOREBOARD

Growth: -
Supporting evidence:
February GDP beat strongly at 0.5% m/m and 1.0% y/y, with services up 0.5% m/m, but the ONS caveat made the print explicitly pre-war. More current signals deteriorated: final March services/composite PMIs fell to 50.5/50.3, all-sector PMI slipped to 49.9, construction PMI stayed in contraction at 45.6, Halifax/RICS/Rightmove all pointed to weaker housing, CBI optimism collapsed to -65, and GfK fell to -25. Retail sales beat at 0.7% m/m, but the ex-fuel gain was only 0.2% and fuel stockpiling drove the headline. The growth tape therefore moved from backward-looking strength to current/forward fragility.

Labor: -
Supporting evidence:
The unemployment rate fell to 4.9%, but the live file treated the move as a false dawn. Payrolls were negative, vacancies fell to a five-year low, inactivity rose, employment growth missed expectations, and private-sector regular pay slowed. The UK labor market did not break abruptly, but the underlying direction was loosening beneath noisy headline optics.

Inflation: -
Supporting evidence:
CPI headline rose to 3.3% y/y and services to 4.5%, but core eased to 3.1% and airfare effects reduced the breadth of the services signal. The more damaging inflation impulse came from the pipeline: PPI input prices jumped 4.4% m/m and 5.4% y/y, PMI input costs reached record pressure, construction input-cost inflation surged, and April flash PMIs showed firms front-loading purchases ahead of feared price rises and supply shortages. Inflation risk therefore deteriorated mainly through cost-push and margin-squeeze channels rather than clean demand overheating.

Financial Conditions: -
Supporting evidence:
The housing channel was the clearest tightening mechanism. Halifax prices fell 0.5% m/m, RICS deteriorated to -23, and Rightmove showed annual asking-price momentum worsening to -0.9%, with mortgage rates explicitly cited as the driver and the average two-year fixed rate at 5.42% versus 4.25% before the shock. Confidence also weakened sharply through GfK and CBI, while PPI and PMI input-cost shocks implied pressure on margins before full consumer pass-through. Financial conditions were therefore tightening through rates, housing, confidence and costs.

Policy Constraint: -
Supporting evidence:
The BoE received no clean signal. Growth and labor softened enough to argue against a fresh hike impulse, but CPI, services inflation, PPI inputs and PMI cost pressure kept the Bank from validating an easy cut narrative. The policy regime is therefore patient hold / cuts delayed / hawkish optionality conditional on pass-through breadth. GBP rate support can persist, but the domestic macro base is increasingly low-quality.
