US GROWTH

The earnings season still argues against an imminent US hard landing. The bank cluster showed capital-markets reopening, stable credit, and active trading; Verizon showed subscriber momentum; Boeing, GE Aerospace and RTX confirmed aerospace / defense demand; Nucor and Texas Instruments showed selective cyclical recovery. The growth signal is positive but barbelled: AI infrastructure, defense, power, capital markets and selected industrial recovery are strong, while fuel-sensitive, rate-sensitive and some consumer-facing areas remain more uneven.

US CONSUMER

The US consumer is intact but bifurcated. American Express shows the premium consumer remains very healthy, with spending acceleration and clean credit. PepsiCo and Procter & Gamble show that the mass consumer is price-sensitive but still responsive to affordability and brand support. Ally pushes back against the auto-credit stress narrative, while Progressive and American Airlines show that insurance and travel demand are still functioning, though fuel and cost pressure are constraining earnings in transport.

FINANCIAL CONDITIONS / CREDIT

Financial conditions are not easy, but they are open enough to sustain activity. JPM, BAC, MS, GS and Citi all showed strong capital-markets and trading activity, while credit remained broadly benign outside specific provisioning noise. Regional and spread-sensitive banks are more mixed: USB looks stable, TFC remains capped by NII/NIM pressure, and PNC is still acquisition-shaped. The financial-system read is not one of credit accident; it is a transition from NII-led earnings to fees, trading, capital markets and selective lending.

AI / CAPEX / INFRASTRUCTURE

AI is now the clearest first-order earnings impulse in the tape. TSM, ASMI, LRCX, TXN, INTC, CDNS, CLS, GEV, VRT and NEE all point to a broadening AI infrastructure cycle across foundry, semicap, analog power, EDA, EMS manufacturing, data-center power, cooling, gas turbines, grid infrastructure and utility origination. The debate is no longer whether AI demand exists; it is whether supply, margins, capital intensity and valuation can keep pace with demand.

EUROPE

Europe looks better than feared in high-quality global franchises. ASMI confirms that European semicap is tightly plugged into the AI capex cycle. L’Oréal delivered a major positive surprise and pushed back against a weak global beauty / China consumer narrative. Eni showed strong upstream production and capital return despite refining drag. Europe is not giving a broad macro boom signal, but select global champions remain resilient and highly investable.

ASIA

Asia remains the hard manufacturing core of the AI and industrial cycle. TSM is the clearest expression of supply-constrained AI demand, while ASMI’s customer base and semicap demand reinforce the same chain. Rio Tinto and BHP suggest China / Asia industrial demand is uneven but not collapsing, with iron ore, copper and long-cycle projects still supported. The Asian signal is stronger in industrial / AI supply chains than in broad consumer demand.

CROSS-ASSET PM TAKE

The strongest earnings impulses are AI infrastructure, capital markets, defense / power, and selective cyclical industry. The main headwinds are Middle East / fuel / logistics shocks, rate-sensitive NII pressure, software margin and cash-flow skepticism, and consumer cost pressure. The tape is not a broad reacceleration boom, but it is also not a hard-landing tape. It is late-cycle resilience with a powerful AI / infrastructure capex overlay.

BOTTOM LINE

Global PM read: no imminent US hard landing; consumer intact but bifurcated; AI now first-order rather than second-order; Europe and Asia have resilient high-quality pockets; Middle East conflict is now visible through fuel, logistics, oilfield disruption and industrial revenue timing. The dominant tension is that institutional / infrastructure earnings are much stronger than household-facing earnings, and the market is only rewarding that growth when capital discipline and margin credibility are also present.
