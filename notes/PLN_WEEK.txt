SIGNAL TENSION CHECK
Main contradiction: CPI at 2.4% y/y and core net inflation at 2.7% y/y both confirmed the most benign domestic price backdrop in the Poland monitoring arc, while wages re-accelerated sharply to 8.6% y/y — a 1.5pp beat versus consensus and 1.5pp acceleration from prior. The disinflation story was real at the headline and pipeline level; the wage story said it might not stay that way.
Why it matters: The NBP's reaction function throughout the monitoring arc consistently weighted wages as the primary domestic inflation risk. A 8.6% y/y wage print — even alongside benign CPI — was the most important single data point for testing whether the NBP could begin easing without reigniting services inflation pressure. The resolution of this tension — whether wages would decelerate toward the 6-7% range consistent with inflation convergence — was the dominant gating variable for the easing cycle entering the next window.
Implication: The February NBP meeting was set up as the first major policy verification point by this window. The committee would need to assess whether the December wage spike was a seasonal distortion or a structural re-acceleration. If January wages confirmed deceleration, the easing case would strengthen materially. If they confirmed persistence at 8%+, the NBP would remain in patience mode despite the benign CPI backdrop.
