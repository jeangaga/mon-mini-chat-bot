A. BRAZIL / BRL — 3-WEEK MACRO SYNTHESIS

Brazil’s three-week tape moved into a softer-growth / still-constrained-inflation regime rather than a clean easing setup. The activity side moderated: services PMI fell to the edge of stagnation, composite slipped below 50, retail y/y slowed sharply, services activity stayed resilient in level terms but softer than expected, and IBC-Br remained positive but decelerating. The growth message is therefore not recession, but fading thrust under high rates.

The policy constraint is that the inflation and external mix did not validate a simple dovish repricing. IGP-DI flipped sharply positive, IPCA beat consensus with food and transport doing most of the work, and later external data showed the current-account deficit wider than expected with FDI below forecast. Brazil is still externally financeable and not in a Turkey-style reserve-stress regime, but the cushion narrowed. For BRL, the tape is mildly negative at the margin: not crisis, not hard landing, but less growth comfort, renewed consumer-price pressure, and a COPOM reaction function that cannot treat soft activity as a clean easing signal.

Growth: -
Supporting evidence:
Brazil’s growth signal weakened without collapsing. Services PMI fell to 50.1 and composite to 49.9, with softer new business and weaker confidence. Services activity later rose only 0.1% m/m versus 0.5% expected, retail sales slowed to just +0.2% y/y, and IBC-Br rose 0.6% m/m but decelerated from January. The aggregate read is positive-but-moderating hard activity with survey deterioration, not reacceleration.

Labor: ~

Inflation: -

Financial Conditions: ~

Policy Constraint: -

Brazil remains “rates biting, but inflation/fiscal/external constraints still dominate.”
