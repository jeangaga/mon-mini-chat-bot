A. BRL WEEK — MACRO SYNTHESIS

Brazil’s week reinforced a constrained-stabilization regime rather than a clean recovery or a clean disinflation continuation. Activity data improved at the margin: manufacturing PMI rose from 47.3 to 49.0 and industrial output posted a second straight monthly gain at 0.9% m/m. But the quality of that improvement remained poor. Industry was still weak in annual and investment-sensitive terms, with capital goods and durable goods deeply negative y/y, while inflation-sensitive pipeline measures re-firmed across IGP-M and IPC-Fipe and PMI cost signals worsened.

That leaves the BCB/BRL mix only marginally improved on growth and more difficult on inflation optics. Fiscal data beat tactically but did not repair the broader credibility bind, and the week as a whole argued against a faster easing path. The right weekly read is high-rate stabilization with worsening price quality and unchanged policy constraint.
