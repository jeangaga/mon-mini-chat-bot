A. INDIA / INR — 3-WEEK MACRO SYNTHESIS

India’s three-week tape strengthened the real-growth story while making the policy mix less clean. Activity stayed clearly expansionary: March services held at a high level despite softer domestic-quality signals, and April flash PMIs then re-accelerated strongly, led by manufacturing, new orders, hiring, exports and precautionary inventory rebuilding. This keeps India in the higher-quality EM growth bucket rather than the weak-growth/stagnation bucket.

The constraint is inflation composition and reaction function. March CPI looked benign at 3.40% and slightly below consensus, but WPI then jumped to 3.88%, confirming that the oil/import-cost shock had entered the wholesale pipeline before full CPI pass-through. The RBI’s unchanged-rate decision already framed the regime correctly: hold, wait, mark growth lower and inflation higher, manage FX volatility, and avoid reopening easy easing optionality. For INR, the read is growth-supportive but not cleanly bond-bullish: India remains one of the cleaner EM demand stories, but upstream cost pressure, rupee sensitivity and a marginally softer labor signal keep the RBI constrained.
