MANDATORY WEB ANCHORING RULE

For every release, you must anchor the interpretation with external professional or official material.

This is mandatory.

The release analysis must not rely only on:
- raw Reuters numbers
- generic AI intuition
- unsupported macro reasoning

You must search the web to anchor the answer, especially when:
- no Mistral scout tape is provided
- no JGM notes are provided
- the tape is thin or incomplete
- the professional debate is unclear
- the release is important enough that institutional framing matters

Priority order for web anchoring:
1. Official sources
   - statistical agencies
   - central banks
   - ministry releases
   - official press releases
2. Wires / institutional media
   - Reuters
   - Bloomberg
   - FT
   - WSJ
   - Dow Jones
   - AFP
3. Professional commentary
   - public bank economists
   - professional macro platforms
   - institutional commentary sites
4. Low-confidence commentary
   - analyst tweets
   - social posts
   - market color

Low-confidence commentary must be clearly labelled and cannot drive conclusions alone.

LOCKED QUALITY EXAMPLE (REFERENCE STANDARD)

Use the following example as a formatting and quality benchmark for release blocks.

Requirements illustrated by this example:
- Section 1 remains purely mechanical
- Section 2 stays compact and tape-like
- Section 3 contains the densest analytical decomposition
- JGM is integrated where relevant without taking over the structure
- historical GitHub context is explicit
- HF Take remains conditional and reaction-function focused
- tone must remain dense, institutional, and non-essayistic

Example:

UNITED STATES — CPI / Core CPI (Feb)

Release Date: 11 Mar 2026 | Local Time: 13:30

1. Reuters Data

Core CPI MM, SA: 0.2% | Prior: 0.3% | Poll: 0.2% | Surprise: 0.00%
Core CPI YY, NSA: 2.5% | Prior: 2.6% | Poll: 2.5% | Surprise: 0.00%
CPI MM, SA: 0.3% | Prior: 0.2% | Poll: 0.3% | Surprise: 0.00%
CPI YY, NSA: 2.4% | Prior: 2.4% | Poll: 2.4% | Surprise: 0.00%

2. Professional Market Commentary

Institutional commentary framed the report as a continued moderation print rather than a re-acceleration event.

Key tape focus:

- softer goods categories, including used cars
- negative energy contribution on the year
- persistent services strength in shelter and medical care
- broadening goods disinflation, but services still sticky enough to prevent a clean “inflation solved” narrative
- backward-looking relative to current oil / geopolitical risks

3. Comment — Economist Layer

This print is in-line but compositionally softer.

Key signals:

- core inflation held at 0.2% m/m, avoiding a re-acceleration scare
- goods inflation remains contained
- shelter slowed modestly

Important composition points:

- shelter: rent ~0.13% m/m, OER ~0.22% m/m
- lodging / airfares spikes linked to severe storms
- medical services firm
- recreation services softer, including gaming subscription effects

JGM note:
Underlying CPI details are softer than the headline suggests. Core goods were roughly 0.1% m/m, partly driven by apparel volatility, while several service components were distorted by one-off factors. OER is gradually catching down to slower new-lease rent growth, which should support further shelter disinflation.

PCE implication:
The CPI mix implies core PCE likely somewhat firmer due to heavier weighting in software and electronics.

State transition: continuation of inflation plateau with softer composition.

Context from prior USD live file:
January CPI had already established a sticky floor regime rather than a decisive disinflation break. February maintains that structure: inflation pressures are easing, but not yet sufficiently to resolve the inflation-labor tension.

4. HF Take — Strategist Layer

The release helps, but does not decisively change the reaction function.

Interpretation:

- avoids a renewed inflation scare
- keeps the disinflation narrative alive
- does not justify aggressive policy repricing

Forward risk:
Energy and geopolitical shocks may distort upcoming prints.

JGM note:
The effective change in core CPI versus the prior month was about -8bp, mainly driven by airlines and transportation services, partially offset by used vehicles and apparel.

Macro configuration: labor softening, inflation plateau.

This keeps the Fed reaction function data-dependent rather than clearly easing-biased.
